#### Why does entropy increase?

At any moment, the representative point of a system is more likely to move towards a more likely micro-state. Therefore, an ordered initial configuration will tend towards equilibrium. 
While it's true that, in principle, by reversing the laws of motion we can decrease entropy, we are effectively picking an unlikely configuration, as most miro-states at equilibrium will usually stay such, as it's the most likely scenario. As we increase the number of variables in the system, these probabilistic arguments only become stronger.
