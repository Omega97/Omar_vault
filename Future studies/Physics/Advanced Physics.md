
## Main Concepts


#### Admitting

Allowing a structure (e.g., a [[Advanced Physics#Metric Tensor|metric]] or [[Advanced Physics#Gauge connections|connection]]) to exist compatibly on a [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifold]] or space, crucial for defining consistent geometric or physical properties.

#### Affine Spaces

Vector spaces without a fixed origin, allowing translations but no absolute reference point. Points are defined relative to each other via vector differences. [[Advanced Physics#Vector bundles|Vector bundles]] often generalize this idea.

#### Associated bundle

A [[Advanced Physics#Fiber bundles|fiber bundle]] constructed from a [[Advanced Physics#Principal bundle|principal bundle]] by attaching a fiber (e.g., a vector space) via a group action, used in [[Advanced Physics#Gauge theory|gauge theories]].

#### Bilinear form

A function that takes two vectors and is linear in each, like the inner product on a vector space or the [[Advanced Physics#Metric Tensor|metric tensor]] on a [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifold]], foundational for geometry and physics.

#### Calabi–Yau manifold

A compact, complex [[Advanced Physics#Kähler manifold|Kähler manifold]] with a vanishing first [[Advanced Physics#Chern class|Chern class]], admitting a [[Advanced Physics#Ricci curvature tensor|Ricci]]-flat [[Advanced Physics#Metric Tensor|metric]]. These manifolds are pivotal in string theory, compactifying extra dimensions (typically from 10 to 4) while preserving supersymmetry.

#### Chern class

A topological invariant of a [[Advanced Physics#Vector bundles|vector bundle]], measuring its “twisting,” used in [[Advanced Physics#Calabi–Yau manifold|complex geometry]] and gauge theories.

#### Choice of connection

Selecting a [[Advanced Physics#Gauge connections|connection]] (e.g., [[Advanced Physics#Levi-Civita connection|Levi-Civita]]) on a [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifold]] or [[Advanced Physics#Fiber bundles|bundle]], defining parallel transport in geometric and physical contexts.

#### CKM Matrix

The Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix, describing quark flavor mixing in weak interactions within the Standard Model, linking quark states via [[Advanced Physics#Gauge theory|gauge symmetries]].

#### Clifford Algebra

An algebra generated by vectors under a quadratic form (e.g., a [[Advanced Physics#Metric Tensor|metric]]), used to define [[Advanced Physics#Spinor|spinors]] and [[Advanced Physics#Dirac operator|Dirac operators]] in quantum field theory.

#### Connection

A mathematical object defining parallel transport of vectors or tensors on a [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifold]] or [[Advanced Physics#Fiber bundles|bundle]], such as in [[Advanced Physics#Gauge theory|gauge theories]].

#### Contraction

Summing over repeated indices of a tensor to reduce its rank, used in operations like forming the [[Advanced Physics#Ricci curvature tensor|Ricci tensor]] from the [[Advanced Physics#Riemannian curvature tensors|Riemann tensor]].

#### Covariant derivative

A derivative on a [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifold]] that respects its geometric structure, preserving [[Advanced Physics#Metric Tensor|metrics]] or [[Advanced Physics#Gauge connections|connections]], used in [[Advanced Physics#Einstein tensor|general relativity]].

#### Differential form

A mathematical object that is antisymmetric under coordinate changes and can be integrated over [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifolds]]. Used to describe fields, such as electromagnetic potentials, in a coordinate-independent way.

#### Dirac operator

A differential operator acting on [[Advanced Physics#Spinor|spinor fields]], crucial in quantum field theory for describing fermion dynamics, such as electrons in relativistic settings.

#### Double cover

A topological construction where a covering space (e.g., [[Advanced Physics#Spin(X,Y)|Spin(X,Y)]]) maps two points to one in the base space (e.g., [[Advanced Physics#SO(1,3)|SO(X,Y)]]), enabling [[Advanced Physics#Spinor|spinor representations]].

#### Einstein’s field equations

A set of equations in general relativity relating the [[Advanced Physics#Einstein tensor|Einstein tensor]] to the energy-momentum tensor, describing how matter and energy curve spacetime via the [[Advanced Physics#Metric Tensor|metric tensor]].

#### Einstein tensor

A tensor derived from the [[Advanced Physics#Metric Tensor|metric]] and [[Advanced Physics#Riemannian curvature tensors|Riemann curvature]], encoding spacetime curvature caused by matter and energy in [[Advanced Physics#Einstein’s field equations|Einstein’s field equations]]. It is a cornerstone of general relativity.

#### Fiber bundles

Spaces that locally resemble a product of a base space and a fiber (e.g., a vector space), used to model structures like [[Advanced Physics#Vector bundles|vector bundles]] or [[Advanced Physics#Principal bundle|principal bundles]] in physics and geometry.

#### Fibration

A continuous map from a total space to a base space, where each point in the base has a fiber (a consistent structure, like a circle or vector space) attached, preserving local geometry.

#### Fundamental Groups

Topological invariants that capture the structure of loops in a space, describing its “holes” in one dimension. They help classify spaces like [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifolds]].

#### Gauge connections

Fields in a [[Advanced Physics#Fiber bundles|fiber bundle]] defining parallel transport, central to [[Advanced Physics#Gauge theory|gauge theories]] like electromagnetism or [[Advanced Physics#Yang-Mills theory|Yang-Mills]].

#### Gauge group

The group of local transformations (e.g., phase changes) leaving a [[Advanced Physics#Gauge theory|gauge theory]] invariant, such as U(1) in electromagnetism or SU(3) in QCD.

#### Gauge theory

A field theory where physical laws are invariant under local [[Advanced Physics#Gauge group|gauge transformations]], describing fundamental interactions like electromagnetism or [[Advanced Physics#Yang-Mills theory|Yang-Mills]].

#### Gauge transformation

A local change of reference frame in a [[Advanced Physics#Gauge theory|gauge theory]], preserving physical predictions, such as shifting the electromagnetic potential.

#### Higgs Mechanism

A process in [[Advanced Physics#Gauge theory|gauge theories]] where [[Advanced Physics#Symmetry breaking|spontaneous symmetry breaking]], via a scalar field, gives mass to [[Advanced Physics#Gauge connections|gauge bosons]], as in the Standard Model.

#### Homology Groups

Algebraic invariants measuring “holes” in a topological space at various dimensions, complementing [[Advanced Physics#Fundamental Groups|fundamental groups]] in classifying spaces.

#### Kähler manifold

A [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifold]] with a compatible complex structure, symplectic structure, and [[Advanced Physics#Metric Tensor|metric]], used in [[Advanced Physics#Calabi–Yau manifold|string theory]] and complex geometry.

#### Levi-Civita connection

The unique torsion-free [[Advanced Physics#Gauge connections|connection]] on a [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifold]] preserving the [[Advanced Physics#Metric Tensor|metric]], used in [[Advanced Physics#Riemannian curvature tensors|Riemannian geometry]].

#### Lie Algebra Cohomology

An algebraic tool measuring invariants of a [[Advanced Physics#Lie Group|Lie group]]’s associated Lie algebra, used in symmetry analysis and [[Advanced Physics#Gauge theory|gauge theories]].

#### Lie Group

A group that is also a smooth [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifold]], where group operations (multiplication, inversion) are smooth maps. Examples include [[Advanced Physics#SO(3)|SO(3)]] and [[Advanced Physics#Lorentz Group|Lorentz Group]].

#### Lorentz Group

The group of transformations preserving the spacetime interval in special relativity, denoted [[Advanced Physics#SO(1,3)|SO(1,3)]]. It describes rotations and boosts in 4D Minkowski spacetime.

#### $M_{PMNS}$ Matrix

The Pontecorvo-Maki-Nakagawa-Sakata matrix, describing neutrino flavor mixing in weak interactions, analogous to the [[Advanced Physics#CKM Matrix|CKM matrix]] for quarks.

#### Manifold

A topological space that locally resembles Euclidean space, enabling calculus in curved settings, such as spacetime in [[Advanced Physics#Einstein tensor|general relativity]] or [[Advanced Physics#Calabi–Yau manifold|Calabi–Yau manifolds]].

#### Metric Tensor

A tensor defining distances, angles, and geometry on a [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifold]]. It underpins [[Advanced Physics#Riemannian curvature tensors|Riemannian geometry]] and [[Advanced Physics#Einstein’s field equations|Einstein’s field equations]].

#### Observers

Entities (or reference frames) assigning coordinates and measurements to physical events in spacetime, often tied to [[Advanced Physics#Lorentz Group|Lorentz transformations]].

#### Principal bundle

A [[Advanced Physics#Fiber bundles|fiber bundle]] with a [[Advanced Physics#Lie Group|Lie group]] as its fiber, acted upon freely by the group, foundational in [[Advanced Physics#Gauge theory|gauge theories]].

#### Pullback

A map transferring geometric objects (e.g., [[Advanced Physics#Differential form|forms]] or [[Advanced Physics#Metric Tensor|tensors]]) from one [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifold]] to another via a smooth function.

#### Renormalization

A technique in quantum field theory to handle infinities by redefining physical quantities (e.g., mass, charge), ensuring finite predictions in theories like [[Advanced Physics#Yang-Mills theory|Yang-Mills]].

#### Ricci curvature tensor

A tensor obtained by [[Advanced Physics#Contraction|contracting]] the [[Advanced Physics#Riemannian curvature tensors|Riemann curvature tensor]], measuring how volumes distort in a [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifold]]. It is critical in [[Advanced Physics#Einstein’s field equations|Einstein’s field equations]].

#### Riemannian curvature tensors

Tensors describing the intrinsic curvature of a [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifold]] with a [[Advanced Physics#Metric Tensor|metric]], used in [[Advanced Physics#Einstein tensor|general relativity]].

#### SO(1,3)

The [[Advanced Physics#Lorentz Group|Lorentz group]] in 4D Minkowski spacetime, preserving the spacetime interval. It is a [[Advanced Physics#Lie Group|Lie group]] central to special relativity.

#### SO(3)

The [[Advanced Physics#Lie Group|Lie group]] of rotations in 3D Euclidean space, describing symmetries of physical systems like angular momentum.

#### Spin(X,Y)

The double cover of the [[Advanced Physics#SO(1,3)|SO(X,Y)]] group, enabling [[Advanced Physics#Spinor representation|spinor representations]] for particles with half-integer spin.

#### Spinor

A mathematical object transforming under [[Advanced Physics#Spin(X,Y)|spin groups]], used to describe fermions (e.g., electrons) in quantum mechanics and relativity.

#### Spinor representation

A linear action of [[Advanced Physics#Spin(X,Y)|Spin groups]] on [[Advanced Physics#Spinor|spinor fields]], crucial for describing fermions in quantum field theory.

#### Symmetry breaking

A process where a system’s symmetry is reduced, often spontaneously, leading to new physical phenomena. It is key in the [[Advanced Physics#Higgs Mechanism|Higgs mechanism]] and phase transitions in [[Advanced Physics#Gauge theory|gauge theories]].

#### Tangent bundle

A [[Advanced Physics#Vector bundles|vector bundle]] where the fiber at each point of a [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifold]] is its tangent space, used to define [[Advanced Physics#Covariant derivative|covariant derivatives]] and study differential geometry.

#### Torsion tensor

A tensor measuring the failure of a [[Advanced Physics#Connection|connection]] to be symmetric, describing twisting in a [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifold]]’s geometry. The [[Advanced Physics#Levi-Civita connection|Levi-Civita connection]] has zero torsion.

#### Vector bundles

A [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifold]] with a vector space attached at each point, generalizing tangent spaces. Used in [[Advanced Physics#Gauge theory|gauge theories]] and [[Advanced Physics#Fiber bundles|fiber bundles]].

## Other Terms

- **Chimeric bundle**: A speculative or niche term, possibly referring to a [[Advanced Physics#Fiber bundles|fiber bundle]] with mixed or hybrid fiber structures, combining features like vector and principal bundles (needs context for precision).
- **Choosing a signature**: Selecting the signature of a [[Advanced Physics#Metric Tensor|metric tensor]] (e.g., (+,−,−,−) for Minkowski spacetime), determining the geometry of a [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifold]].
- **Frobenius Inner Product**: The inner product on a [[Advanced Physics#Clifford Algebra|Clifford algebra]] or matrix algebra, often used to define norms or traces in geometric and algebraic contexts.
- **Frobenius metric**: A [[Advanced Physics#Metric Tensor|metric]] compatible with a Frobenius manifold, where the curvature satisfies specific algebraic conditions, often studied in topological field theory.
- **Observation map**: A map assigning measurement outcomes to geometric or physical data, often in the context of [[Advanced Physics#Observers|observers]] or [[Advanced Physics#Gauge theory|field theories]].
- **Pati-Salam**: A grand unified theory extending the Standard Model, unifying quarks and leptons under a larger [[Advanced Physics#Gauge group|gauge group]] (e.g., SU(4)×SU(2)×SU(2)).
- **Passing to the double cover**: Transitioning from a group (e.g., [[Advanced Physics#SO(1,3)|SO(1,3)]]) to its [[Advanced Physics#Double cover|double cover]] (e.g., [[Advanced Physics#Spin(X,Y)|Spin(1,3)]]) to enable [[Advanced Physics#Spinor representation|spinor representations]].
- **Pseudo-tensor**: An object transforming like a tensor under specific coordinate changes (e.g., proper Lorentz transformations), but not others, unlike true tensors in [[Advanced Physics#Einstein tensor|general relativity]].
- **Pulling back**: Using a map to transfer geometric objects, like [[Advanced Physics#Differential form|forms]] or [[Advanced Physics#Metric Tensor|tensors]], from one [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifold]] to another, preserving structure.
- **Space of metrics**: The set of all possible [[Advanced Physics#Metric Tensor|metric tensors]] on a [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifold]], used to study geometric variations, as in [[Advanced Physics#Einstein tensor|general relativity]].
- **Structure Tensor**: A tensor encoding algebraic or geometric properties, such as torsion or curvature, in structures like [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifolds]] or [[Advanced Physics#Lie Group|Lie groups]].
- **Trace-reversing**: Subtracting the trace of a tensor (e.g., [[Advanced Physics#Metric Tensor|metric]] or [[Advanced Physics#Einstein tensor|Einstein tensor]]) to obtain its traceless part, often used in [[Advanced Physics#Einstein tensor|general relativity]].
- **Vacuum expectation value**: The average value of a quantum field in its lowest-energy (vacuum) state, critical in the [[Advanced Physics#Higgs Mechanism|Higgs mechanism]] and quantum field theory.
- **Weyl spinors**: Two-component [[Advanced Physics#Spinor|spinors]] describing massless fermions, transforming under the [[Advanced Physics#Spin(X,Y)|Spin group]], used in chiral theories.
- **Yang-Mills theory**: A [[Advanced Physics#Gauge theory|gauge theory]] with non-Abelian [[Advanced Physics#Gauge group|gauge groups]], describing fundamental interactions like the strong force in quantum chromodynamics.


#### Vial spinor

A hypothetical or context-specific extension of [[Advanced Physics#Spinor|spinor]] fields, possibly referring to spinors defined over vial-like structures (e.g., in higher-dimensional or branched [[Advanced Physics#Manifold|manifolds]]) or in speculative gauge theories beyond the Standard Model.

#### SO(10)

A [[Advanced Physics#Lie Group|Lie group]] of 10-dimensional orthogonal transformations with positive determinant, used in grand unified theories (GUTs) to unify the Standard Model gauge groups ([[Advanced Physics#Gauge group|SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1)]]) into a single representation, accommodating quarks, leptons, and their interactions in 16-dimensional [[Advanced Physics#Spinor representation|spinor representations]].

#### Spin-10

The double cover of the [[Advanced Physics#SO(10)|SO(10)]] group, denoted Spin(10), enabling [[Advanced Physics#Spinor representation|spinor representations]] in 10 dimensions; central to SO(10) GUTs for describing chiral fermions and supersymmetric extensions in string theory.
