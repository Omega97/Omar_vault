
## No-Limit Five-card Obama: A Betting and Climbing Game 🃏

**Three-card Obama** is a rapid-fire betting game for 2–6 players that combines the bluffing of Poker with the tactical card-shedding of games like Big Two.

---

### **🛠️ The Setup**

- **Deck:** Two standard 52-card decks shuffled together (104 cards total, no Jokers).
    
- **Rank Order:** $2, 3, 4, \dots, J, Q, K, A$  
    
- **Deal:** Each player receives **9 cards**.

---

### **💰 Phase 1: The Discard & The Bet**

1. **Selection:** Players choose exactly **5 cards** to keep and discard the remaining face-down.
    
2. **Wagering:** Every player bets a one-point blind. Then, starting from the dealer, they proceed with a standard **betting round of no-limit Poker**, where they can check, bet, raise, call, and fold.

---

### **⚔️ Phase 2: The Climb**

If multiple players remain, the "Climbing" phase begins to determine who wins the pot.

1. **The Lead:** The Dealer reveals the top card from the deck instead. This is the "Starter".
    
2. **The Rules of Play:** Moving clockwise, players must either **Pass** or play a better combination than the previous player. You can beat the previous play in two ways:
    
    - 🃏 **A single card** on **any combination of (strictly) lower rank**.
        
    - 🃏🃏 **A pair** on **any single card** of different rank.
        
    - 🃏🃏🃏 **Any triplet** on **any pair** of different rank.
      
3. **Clearing**: When all players but one **pass**, the board is cleared, and that same player may play **any set** (combination of cards of the same rank) to the field. 
     
4. **Winning:** The first player to **clear their entire hand**, **wins the pot**.

Notes: 
- *If all players pass at the beginning of the hand, then the board is also cleared, and it's the Dealer's turn to play.*
- *the rules strike a delicate balance, where the first player has an advantage, but is not too dominant, and big hands can't always be played at theri fullest, thus giving away information to the other players.* 

---

### **🧠 Strategy Tips**

- **The Power of Three:** Triplets are very strong, especially in a late position, but you may have to break them if no one plays a pair.
    
- **Position is King:** Being the last to act in the climbing phase is a massive advantage.
    
- **The Singleton Trap:** It is very unlikely to win with only singletons, as any pair will beat you.

---

### **⚡ Speed Play Rule**

To keep the game "rapid-fire," do not reshuffle the decks after every hand. Simply discard the used cards and continue dealing until the decks are entirely consumed.
